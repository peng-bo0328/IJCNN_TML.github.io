About This Special Session

Motivation

Deployed ML systems increasingly operate under dataset shift, partial observability, distributional drift, and adversarial pressure. In safety-critical contexts, these systems must be reliable, robust, fair, private, and accountable.

Objectives

This special session brings together advances in theory, algorithms, and applications to connect principled risk bounds with scalable methods and field results, spotlighting evaluation protocols that reflect operational realities.

Novelty

Our session uniquely bridges theory, algorithm and application, addressing urgent needs in the era of foundation models which often demonstrate poor trustworthiness in real-world environments.

Topics of Interest

Distribution Shift

OOD detection, open-set recognition, OOD generalization, test-time adaptation, test-time training

Robust Learning

Adversarial robustness, poisoning and backdoor defenses

Model Uncertainty Estimation

Bayesian deep learning, conformal prediction, selective classification

Autonomous Trustworthy ML

Hyperparameter optimization and neural architecture search

Trustworthy Foundation Models

Jailbreak and prompt-injection defenses, data poisoning and backdoors, hallucination detection/mitigation

Evaluation

Benchmark datasets, platforms and measures to evaluate trustworthiness in real-world contexts

Potential Impact

Research

Clearer links between theory and practice; standardized evaluation under distribution shift.

Practice

Recipes for rollout safety and monitoring for ML reliability, and governance frameworks that reduce risk and compliance burden.

Society

Improved fairness, privacy, and safety characteristics in high-stakes deployments, increasing public trust in ML technologies.

Community

Open datasets/tools, shared testbeds, and cross-disciplinary collaborations seeded by the session.
