WCCI 2026 Special Session on

Toward Trustworthy Machine Learning in the Wild:

Theory, Algorithm and Application

21 - 26 June 2026, Maastricht, The Netherlands

Overview

Deployed machine learning (ML) systems increasingly operate under dataset shift, partial observability, distributional drift, and adversarial pressure. In safety- and mission-critical contexts (healthcare, finance, autonomy, climate risk, scientific discovery), these systems must be reliable, robust, fair, private, and accountable—not just accurate on static benchmarks. Despite rapid advances, real-world deployments still face:

This special session bridges theory, algorithms, and applications to close the gap between principled guarantees and field deployment. We also spotlight trustworthy use of foundation models (hallucinations, jailbreaks, prompt‑injection, data poisoning/backdoors) and evaluation protocols that reflect operational realities.

Submission Guidelines

Each paper is limited to 8 pages, including figures, tables, references, and appendices. A maximum of two extra pages per paper is allowed (i.e, up to 10 pages), at an additional charge of 100 $ per extra page.

Please submit through the the IEEE WCCI 2026 online submission system and select this special session under the list of research topics in the submission system. Submissions must conform to the author guidelines available on the WCCI2026 website at: https://attend.ieee.org/wcci 2026/information-for-authors/.

Submissions should be original and not currently under review at another venue. All submissions will be reviewed and will be evaluated on the basis of their technical content. Accepted papers will appear in the WCCI proceedings.

To help ensure correct formatting, please use the IEEE style files for conference proceedings as a template for your submission. These include LaTeX and Word style files.

In order to implement the double-anonymous policy, please use the following author statement in the latex file: \author{\IEEEauthorblockN{Anonymous Authors}}.

If you use Word, just use "Anonymous Authors” as authors’ names.

If you have any questions or difficulty submitting your paper, contact us at bopeng.ml@gmail.com

Topics of Interest (include but are not limited to)

  • Distribution shift: OOD detection, open‑set recognition, OOD generalization, test‑time training/adaptation
  • Robust learning: adversarial robustness; poisoning and backdoor defenses
  • Uncertainty & calibration: Bayesian deep learning, conformal prediction, selective classification
  • Autonomous trustworthy machine learning: hyperparameter optimization, neural architecture search
  • Trustworthy foundation models: jailbreak/prompt‑injection defenses; data poisoning/backdoors; hallucination mitigation
  • Evaluation: datasets, platforms, and measures for trustworthiness under real‑world shift
  • Applications & AI4Science: biology, physics, chemistry, materials, climate, healthcare, autonomy, time‑series, control, vision & language
